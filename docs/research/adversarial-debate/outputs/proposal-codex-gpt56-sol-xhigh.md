# Filed Conviction: Opportunistic Insider-Purchase Clusters, Not Another Momentum Patch

## Thesis (1 paragraph)

Stop optimizing Core. Its long-run signal drift is mostly common beta, its cross-sectional increment is thin, its ranking loses to the naïve §2.5 control, and its apparent profitable exits were contaminated by corporate-action forced closes. The highest-information next bet is a **new event edge**: buy liquid US common stocks only after the public Form 4 record reveals a cluster of at least two distinct, ex-ante-classified opportunistic officers/directors making qualifying non-derivative code-P acquisitions, enter at the next regular-session open, and hold for a predeclared 60 sessions without a price stop or take-profit. This is not “insiders always win”; it is a narrow test of whether costly, discretionary, independently repeated informed purchases identify the small subset of firms whose slow information diffusion survives public filing, opening gaps, execution costs, factor adjustment, inactive-security outcomes, and modern walk-forward testing. Until the filing-time event study clears the gates below, no portfolio optimization is justified.

## Mechanism (why edge should exist economically)

Corporate officers and directors already have large, undiversified exposure to employer risk. A discretionary cash acquisition adds more exposure and therefore conveys more information than a sale, which may fund consumption, tax, diversification, or an automatic plan. Requiring two distinct people reduces the chance that the observation is one person's liquidity choice. Requiring both to be “opportunistic” using only their prior three complete years removes much calendar-routine trading. The return should accrue slowly because a Form 4 discloses an action, not the private state that motivated it; outside investors face adverse-selection uncertainty, analyst/institutional attention is weakest where insiders are most informative, and limits to arbitrage make immediate full incorporation costly.

This mechanism makes sharp predictions. Purchase clusters should beat single purchases; opportunistic clusters should beat routine clusters; the spread should remain after market, industry, size, value, and momentum adjustment; and the effect should survive when every event is timed from public availability rather than transaction date. If those orderings fail, the “informed conviction” story is wrong even if a raw long-only backtest rises with the market.

## Concrete strategy design (entries, ranking, exits, portfolio, costs assumptions)

**Public-time data contract.** Ingest immutable SEC ownership filings plus acceptance metadata. The event object is bitemporal: transaction date is `valid_time`; EDGAR acceptance/observed publication is `known_time`. Form 4/A is a prospective partial correction, never a rewrite of the original signal. Map CIK, security title, and event-date symbol to one period-valid listed share class; quarantine ambiguity. The SEC says code P can mean an open-market **or private** purchase, so the machine-only signal is honestly named a “Form-4 code-P Table-I acquisition.” Call it open-market only when footnotes or a verified vendor field prove that venue. Official semantics: [SEC Form 4 instructions](https://www.sec.gov/files/form4data%2C0.pdf), [ownership transaction codes](https://www.sec.gov/edgar/searchedgar/ownershipformcodes.html), and [Insider Transactions datasets](https://www.sec.gov/data-research/sec-markets-data/insider-transactions-data-sets).

**Primary event.** A listed US primary common stock passes the prior-session liquidity floor of price at least $5 and 20-session median dollar volume at least $5 million. A qualifying buyer is an officer or director reporting a positive, non-derivative Table-I acquisition with `TRANS_CODE=P` and `ACQUIRED_DISPOSED=A`; pure 10% owners, derivatives, awards, exercises, tax dispositions, invalid prices/shares, and ambiguous security mappings are excluded. Direct ownership is primary; indirect ownership is a preregistered sensitivity.

At each January 1, classify an insider from only the three previous complete calendar years. “Routine” requires at least one trade in the same calendar month in all three years; adequate-history insiders not meeting that pattern are “opportunistic”; everyone else is unclassified and excluded. A cluster is complete only when filings from **two distinct opportunistic people** at the same issuer become public within ten trading sessions. Multiple rows, joint reports, and amendments do not create extra buyers. Collapse repeat clusters for the issuer during an active holding period.

**Entry.** Always enter at the next regular-session open after the filing date that makes the second qualifying buyer observable. This deliberately gives up same-day reaction and avoids unverifiable “available before close” assumptions. If the next open is halted or absent, obey a deterministic no-fill rule; never substitute the prior close.

**Ranking.** The position-blind primary study includes every qualifying cluster. If the portfolio lacks capacity, rank only by predeclared mechanism variables: distinct qualifying-buyer count descending, aggregate disclosed purchase dollars divided by prior-day median dollar volume descending, then prior-day dollar volume descending; ticker is the deterministic final tie-breaker. No price momentum, 52-week-high, composite, or trained score is allowed in the primary specification.

**Exit.** Exit at the open after 60 completed trading sessions. No price stop, take-profit, or trailing exit is allowed in the primary test: the existing evidence says the first five sessions are flat/negative, the ±1 ATR race is worse than a coin flip, and drift appears later. Twenty- and 120-session exits are diagnostics in the declared multiple-testing family, not a license to select the prettiest horizon. A merger, cash-out, delisting, or other corporate action receives its actual economic consideration; a policy blocker cannot manufacture a profitable forced close. Missing terminal economics invalidates the run.

**Portfolio.** Long-only, maximum 20 concurrent names, inverse-20-day-volatility sizing, 5% equity cap per issuer, 100% gross cap, and order notional capped at 1% of prior-session median dollar volume. When the cap binds, use the ranking above. Report equal-event results for signal diagnosis and capacity-weighted results for deployability. Factor/industry-adjusted alpha, not unhedged beta, is the evidence of selection edge.

**Costs.** Primary metric is pre-tax after 5 bps per side, including the realized next-open gap; tax remains secondary. Stress at 10 and 25 bps per side, report no-fill and participation assumptions, and reject any claim that exists only at zero cost or by trading the filing-date close.

## How it differs from current Core / §2.5 control

Core and §2.5 infer continuation from the same object—price/volume path—then differ mainly in selection complexity. This proposal changes the information source. The binary event is a public, costly action by informed agents; price history supplies only liquidity, sizing, and risk controls. Core's momentum → 52-week-high → liquidity ranking disappears. The §2.5 spike trigger disappears. The structural/tight stop and 2×ATR take-profit disappear from the primary signal test. The inherited machinery is the PIT universe, daily bars, next-open execution, replay, corporate-action handling, fixed costs, and reporting—not the failed signal logic.

## Why prior experiments predict this might work (map to Steps 0–3)

**Step 0:** Core's uncapped raw PF of 0.972 and negative after-cost expectancy show that capital allocation did not hide a good price signal. The new proposal does not rescale that signal; it replaces it. The 3,413 stop losses and near-immediate stop timing argue against using the existing 1×ATR path on any slow-information event.

**Step 1:** The +$1.03 million forced-close contribution was a kind-blind corporate-action artifact. Form 4 research is especially exposed to small/inactive issuers, so acceptance explicitly requires real terminal consideration, a bitemporal action model, and zero profit attributed to context-policy liquidation.

**Step 2:** Core events show no positive short-horizon mean at +1/+5 days but positive +10 to +60-day drift; however, +20-day excess versus the same-date eligible universe is only +0.36% with clustered t=1.78. That combination says “use a slow horizon, but bring a stronger cross-sectional information source.” A 60-session insider horizon respects the observed path without relabeling beta as alpha.

**Step 3:** The more elaborate Core ranking lost to the naïve control OOS (+30 versus −18 expectancy per trade in the reported capped runs). Therefore no new continuous price score deserves another search budget. A two-person opportunistic-purchase cluster is a predeclared economic interaction with explicit routine and single-buyer controls. It earns its complexity only if those controls order monotonically out of sample.

The corrected 2022–2025 matrix produced no valid P&L because stale terminal opens correctly failed closed. Nothing here treats that absent evidence as confirmation; the new data path must pass the same safeguard before returns count.

## Why it might fail (steelman opposition)

1. **Public filing may be too late.** Insiders trade first; the market and specialist data feeds may absorb most information before a next-open follower can act.
2. **Old-sample anomaly decay.** The canonical evidence is mostly pre-2010. Faster post-SOX disclosure, alternative data, and systematic Form 4 products may have arbitraged it away.
3. **Contrarian/value disguise.** Insiders buy after declines. Apparent alpha may be size, value, illiquidity, or ordinary reversal rather than private information.
4. **Code-P ambiguity.** Without reliable footnotes, private purchases contaminate the purported open-market signal. Form 4/A partial amendments and joint reports create duplicate/correction traps.
5. **Small-stock mirage.** The strongest effect may live where spreads, halts, no-fills, manipulation, and delisting omissions make it untradeable. Percentage alpha need not scale to dollar profit.
6. **Classifier and cluster scarcity.** Three prior years plus two distinct opportunistic buyers may leave a small, nonrepresentative sample. Relaxing it after seeing results would be p-hacking.
7. **No-stop tail risk.** A 60-session hold admits gaps and permanent impairment. Volatility sizing reduces but does not remove single-name crash risk.
8. **Data dependency risk.** The repo currently proves SEP+ACTIONS+TICKERS, not an entitled, timestamped, versioned insider feed. A vendor's “daily” table may be ex-post clean rather than as-known-then.
9. **Regime dependence.** Insider buying may merely load on recoveries and easy financial conditions, recreating the same 2021–2025 instability in a different costume.
10. **Legal signal, crowded implementation.** The disclosure is public, but the economically useful subset may be too rare and too crowded at the open to support this portfolio.

## Measurement plan (ordered experiments, sample design, stats)

1. **Freeze the protocol before acquiring returns.** Commit the exact transaction fields, classifier, ten-session cluster, next-open rule, 60-session primary horizon, liquidity floor, control groups, costs, folds, and gates. Register every later deviation.
2. **Build and audit the PIT event tape.** Preserve raw accessions and acceptance metadata; reconcile a stratified sample to SEC filings; test duplicate owners/rows, Form 4/A partial corrections, late filings, 10b5-1 fields, CIK/share-class linkage, current-ticker leakage, and vendor/SEC disagreements. Require inactive securities and terminal consideration. No strategy run until the data gate passes.
3. **Run a position-blind filing-time event study.** From next open, measure +1/+5/+10/+20/+60/+120 session raw and 5-bps-per-side returns, same-date eligible-universe excess returns, industry/characteristic-matched returns, and pre-event-estimated factor residuals. One issuer event may enter only once per 60 sessions. Cluster by availability date and issuer; use a date-block/wild bootstrap because horizons overlap.
4. **Test the mechanism, not a kitchen sink.** Primary contrast: opportunistic two-buyer cluster versus routine two-buyer cluster. Secondary ordered contrasts: two-buyer versus single-buyer opportunistic events, and buyer-count monotonicity. Use identical liquidity and timing rules. Add shuffled filing-date and matched post-decline placebos. If routine, single, and shuffled events look the same, stop.
5. **Walk forward.** Use expanding three-year classification history and annual test folds; all labels, liquidity, mappings, and factor exposures are formed only from data known before the test fold. The already examined 2023–2025 period is development/walk-forward evidence, never a fresh holdout. Freeze once, then reserve the next genuinely future period.
6. **Correct the research family.** Treat every horizon, liquidity cut, ownership type, role filter, classifier, weighting, and cost scenario tried as one family. Use Romano–Wolf/max-t stepdown or an equivalent block-bootstrap reality check; also report deflated Sharpe for portfolio variants. Preserve null and failed trials.
7. **Only after signal acceptance, run the portfolio replay.** Compare the fixed 60-session policy with the same event set under no position cap and under the declared 20-name capacity. Then compare Core and §2.5 over the identical valid calendar interval and execution/corporate-action engine. Keep tests sequential: the 16 GB host must never load two full fixtures concurrently.
8. **Run adversarial attribution.** Remove policy forced closes; include actual delisting/merger proceeds; decompose by year, liquidity, market cap proxy, role, and entry gap; jackknife issuers/dates/years; report capacity and the worst fold. A profitable mean with an untradeable liquid subset fails.

## Acceptance / rejection gates

**Data acceptance before economics:** at least 95% of sampled qualifying events reconcile to immutable source filings and unique period-valid securities; zero detected lookahead entries or retrospective amendment rewrites; every held inactive security has explicit terminal economics. Otherwise reject the dataset, not merely the strategy run.

**Signal acceptance:** the preregistered +60-session, next-open, 5-bps-per-side abnormal return is positive and its family-wise-adjusted 95% confidence interval excludes zero; it is positive in a majority of annual walk-forward folds; the opportunistic-cluster spread over routine clusters is positive; and the two-buyer contrast beats single-buyer events in the predicted direction. Raw absolute return without abnormal-return evidence fails.

**Economic acceptance:** the capacity-weighted portfolio has positive pre-tax after-cost expectancy, PF at least about 1.10 across a broad, predeclared liquidity/cost region, positive factor alpha with dependence-robust evidence, and positive economics at both 5 and 10 bps per side. No single trade or calendar year may supply more than roughly 25% of total profit. The result must remain positive in the liquid subset and after removing every policy forced close.

**Reject immediately** if alpha exists only from transaction-date/filing-close entry, only in illiquid microcaps, only before costs, only at one selected horizon, only under ex-post-cleaned amendments/current identifiers, only with omitted delistings, or only before multiple-testing correction. Reject the mechanism if routine clusters or shuffled-date placebos perform comparably. Reject deployment if the next untouched holdout fails, even if development statistics pass.

## Confidence (0–100) and single sharpest risk

**Confidence: 63/100.** The literature and economic mechanism are much stronger than another price-ranking tweak, and the test is brutally falsifiable. The single sharpest risk is **filing-time alpha decay**: the old transaction-time effect may already be incorporated by the next tradable open, leaving only illiquid, non-scalable remnants.

## Attacks on the other two archetypes

A) **Hold longer / widen path — keep breakout signal, fix horizon & stops only.** This is the best diagnostic and a weak strategy thesis. The event study undeniably says the current stop censors later drift, so one constant-risk, stop-free fixed-hold control should be run. But +20-day excess is only +0.36% with t=1.78 and negative median excess; the high +60/+120 absolute means can be common beta. Removing stops mechanically increases time in market and can turn a failed selector into a bull-market clone. If a matched-universe or factor-residual portfolio is not positive after costs across folds, wider path has explained the loss mechanism without discovering alpha.

Claude's **Quiet Drift** is the strongest version of A because it demands the missing h60 excess-return measurement before engine work. That cheap falsification should be run. Its proposed edge, however, is not the published Da–Gurun–Warachka experiment: the paper defines information discreteness over a prior 12-month formation period and double-sorts on formation return, whereas Quiet Drift substitutes a trailing 60-session measure inside an already spike-conditioned cohort, then retains 52-week-high proximity and adds a 95th-percentile gap penalty. “Nearly orthogonal” is asserted, not measured. Top-quintile admission on the same burned fixture can therefore become a three-feature ranking search wearing a one-feature label. If h60 excess is zero, Quiet Drift is dead; if h60 excess is positive but ID is not monotone on frozen folds, the correct result is the unranked fixed-hold control—not another percentile or lookback.

B) **Ranking overhaul — continuous scores, deciles, composite; kill binary accept.** Core's momentum → high proximity → liquidity ordering already added sophistication and lost to the naïve control. A continuous composite multiplies analyst degrees of freedom across transforms, weights, cutoffs, neutralization, and deciles while the measured CS increment is thin. Monotone deciles would diagnose information, but optimizing a score before any component shows stable conditional expectancy is p-hacking with better charts. Quiet Drift is admissible only as one frozen-feature ablation; the moment its 60-day adaptation, high-proximity tiebreak, gap penalty, quintile cut, or 40/60/80-day horizon is tuned from this fixture, it becomes the archetype it claims to avoid.

C) **Regime / risk-on gate — 200DMA / breadth / vol filter before any signal.** There are only a few independent regimes in 2019–2025, and the negative 2021–2022/2025 versus positive 2023–2024 pattern makes it trivial to draw a gate around history. A 200DMA or breadth rule can improve a long-only backtest merely by reducing bear-market beta, not by improving signal selection; cash is then the hidden benchmark. With overlapping long holds, the effective regime sample is smaller still. A regime variable belongs in attribution and risk budgeting after signal alpha, not as a rescue filter. It must predict the cross-sectional residual, not just market direction.

Grok's **DAMB** compounds that problem. Its TSMOM anchor is strongest across liquid futures and does not prove that `stock > 200DMA + 52-week-high proximity + 20-day breakout` selects individual equities after costs. Its supposedly decisive E1 is not a clean horizon test: it retains the structural price stop while simultaneously removes the take-profit and introduces 20/40-day trails plus a 90-day cap. A failure would not falsify stop-free long-horizon collection; a success would not identify which change helped. Then DAMB adds a 93/95/97 proximity grid, alternative stock-trend definitions, a hard SPY 200DMA gate, trail and time-stop grids, volatility targets, and position-count variants. Deflated Sharpe cannot restore information destroyed by fitting that many correlated choices to roughly seven years and only a few market cycles. Its fill ordering is also economically a ranking whenever the cap binds, whatever label “capital rationing” gives it.

**Most dangerous: C, as instantiated by DAMB.** Generic B can waste unlimited trials, but Quiet Drift at least offers a one-day kill test. DAMB can produce a persuasive green backtest simply by combining longer beta exposure with an ex-post-friendly market gate and many path knobs; the result may look risk-managed while establishing neither stock-selection alpha nor a clean causal contribution. That is the highest-probability route from the present evidence to false deployment confidence.

## Final one-liner

I would bet the next research budget on a PIT tape of two-person opportunistic Form-4 purchase clusters, and the single proof is a preregistered next-open +60-session event study whose 5-bps-after-cost factor-adjusted return stays positive with a family-wise-adjusted confidence interval above zero in the liquid walk-forward sample.
