## Thesis (1 paragraph)

The human Full-Stop seal and ADR-0001 correctly convert those joint nulls into a **CS alpha budget kill**, not a soft pause waiting for curiosity. The strongest next path is therefore **not** another CS line: hold Full-Stop without exception, put production capital on **honest liquid beta only**, and spend the calendar quarter on **non-claim capital productization** (vehicle, custody/execution, tax-lot discipline, risk budget, monitoring) with explicit anti-theater rules so allocation is never rebranded as discovered edge. Re-opening PEAD tape, Form-4 F0, or residual knobs would launder the same research-economics failure mode R2 already paid to kill: engineering weeks before a falsifiable number, density/year-share risk, and silent override of freeze law. Full-Stop is a **process success**. Defend it.

## Mechanism (why this path is correct economically / process-wise)

**Research economics first.** In Round 2 I argued for the cheapest high-breadth falsifier on existing tape (xs-reversal). Meta-judge funded that first; measurement returned kill_line. PEAD F0 was the denser “new info” backup; the object required to even *start* returns was absent, and fail-closed protocol correctly refused to invent coverage. Form-4 remains unstarted by design (sparse, high ontology/year-share risk, dual-exit later). After two sequential funded nulls plus residual DIE, the *marginal* research dollar on CS alpha has negative expected process value: each re-open pays setup tax (tape, mapping, known_time, integrity) before the first number that can kill. Opportunity cost of those engineering weeks is first-class evidence (R3 principle 12–13), not a narrative inconvenience.

**Capital ≠ alpha research.** Sharpe’s arithmetic still binds the average active dollar after costs. When every predeclared CS claim on this stack has failed honest gates (promotion, residual economic bar, reverse spread, PEAD data object), the default production book is market exposure without a CS story. Ibbotson–Kaplan style allocation literature places the dominant return variability in policy mix over time—not in paper cross-section books that never clear concentration, median, or matched-exposure bars. Productizing beta is **capital honesty**, not a new signal product and not residual packaging.

**Freeze law is law.** Hard/narrow residual freeze, program kills on R2-1 and PEAD, and Full-Stop budget kill are distinct layers (CONTEXT glossary). Conflating “program kill of PEAD under protocol X” with “we still owe Form-4 because meta ordered R2-3” is the soft-override trap. Event-only re-open requires new PRD + grill + named hypothesis—this Round 3 debate is allowed as process stress-test; it is **not** automatic permission to spend on tape. Holding Full-Stop *is* the dual-exit success mode R2-4 already named.

**Joint evidence, not cherry-pick.** Gate 1a PASS (mean h60 excess, median still negative) never was go-live. Phase 2 NO-GO (2020 ≈ 85.7% profit share) and residual DIE (leave-2020 mean ≤ half full-book; S2 trade−SPY mean excess < 0) kill portfolio residual hope. R2-1 fails gross t, year share, net, and several fail-closed gaps. PEAD fails before returns. Together they do not say “try Form-4 next Friday”; they say the CS alpha budget earned its Full-Stop.

## Concrete program design (capital, research, engineering — what happens in what order)

### Track 0 — Production capital (this quarter; primary budget)

Ordered, non-claim capital productization. No accept path from research artifacts. No `capital_go` flip from CS reports.

1. **Policy write-down (week 1):** Written IPS-style note: target is liquid equity (and optional cash buffer) market exposure; no CS alpha overlay; no residual book; no “paper long-short until live.” Single page, human-owned.
2. **Vehicle selection (week 1–2):** Choose one primary liquid beta vehicle (e.g. broad equity ETF or brokerage index product available to the operator). Criteria: liquidity, total expense, tax domicile fit, tracking honesty. **Not** a multi-factor smart-beta “alpha-lite” product dressed as beta unless explicitly labeled non-claim and still measured only as allocation.
3. **Custody / execution path (week 2–3):** Broker account, funding, recurring contribution or lump, explicit rebalance rule (calendar or band). Document fill assumptions; no research-backtest theater.
4. **Tax-lot and cost discipline (week 3–4):** Lot method, wash-sale awareness where relevant, contribution schedule. Engineering only as needed for personal ops—not a trading stack re-architecture.
5. **Risk and drawdown protocol (ongoing):** Max equity fraction, cash floor, no leverage claim. Monitoring is NAV vs benchmark tracking, not promotion gates.
6. **Anti-theater checklist (gate on every PR):** Diff must not create accept path, unfreeze residual, re-run curiosity backtests, or smuggle TSMOM/factor timing as “honest beta.”

## Why it might fail (steelman opposition)

1. **True edge lives in unbuilt tapes.** PEAD original-as-published or Form-4 filing-time could still clear dense gates if funded. Holding Full-Stop then leaves money on the table for months.
2. **Beta productization is boring and can stall.** Without a research drumbeat, the operator may neither allocate capital nor improve tooling—Full-Stop becomes inertia, not honesty.
3. **Opportunity cost of *not* learning ownership/fundamentals plumbing.** Even non-alpha engineering of SF1/Form-4 adapters might later reduce re-open cost; never building raises future re-open tax.
4. **Regime change.** 2019–2025 fixture nulls may not describe a future high-dispersion regime where residual or reverse spreads revive.
5. **Political failure mode of Full-Stop.** Teams re-label work as “non-claim” while building accept paths; seal erodes without a living anti-theater gate.

**Rebuttal (not denial):** (1) is exactly why re-open is event-only with density first—not why we auto-spend after two kills. (2) is fixed by making capital product the *only* funded track with weekly ops checklist. (3) adapters without a named hypothesis are curiosity; if human wants plumbing, fund it as **non-claim infrastructure with no E1 path** under a separate written scope—not PEAD-for-alpha. (4) regime hope without a PRD is residual theater. (5) is process risk; checklist + ADR + no research driver for CS lines mitigate it.

## Attacks on the other two archetypes

Attack alternative directions even where partial agreement exists. Rank danger after attacks.

### A) Hold Full-Stop + productize honest liquid beta — stop CS alpha; capital is allocation

**This is my primary path—attack the *weak* versions so meta cannot approve a soft A.**

**Still correct core:** capital/research split; Full-Stop as success; no residual packaging.

### B) Explicit re-open PEAD — fund SF1/SEC original-as-published tape + measured F0 → E1 only if pass

- **PEAD F0 already published kill_line.** D1–D7 fail-closed because the object is missing—not because gates were soft. “Fund the tape” is exactly the engineering tax Full-Stop was sealed to stop *without a new PRD*.
